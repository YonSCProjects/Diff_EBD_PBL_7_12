R4 — Safety Reminder

Project 1 • Light Signals
Project 1 is very safe. The Arduino runs on 5 volts, which is too low to hurt you. But you can still break things if you wire something wrong. Read these three rules and you will be fine.

Rule 1 — Never connect 5 V directly to GND

Do not put a jumper wire straight from 5 V to GND. This is called a short circuit. It will make the Arduino reset itself or turn off to protect itself. No one gets hurt, but the circuit stops working until you remove the bad wire.

The 5 V pin powers the LEDs, sensors, and buttons you connect. The GND pin is the return path for the electricity. They should never touch each other directly.

Rule 2 — Every LED needs a resistor

Do not connect an LED directly to a 5 V pin with no resistor. The LED will light up very brightly for a second or two, get hot, and burn out. You will hear the teacher say "that's why we use a resistor" and then you will need a new LED.

Every LED in Project 1 has a 220 Ω resistor (colour bands: red, red, brown) between its long leg and the Arduino pin. If you see an LED in your circuit without a resistor, fix it before you upload anything.

Rule 3 — USB cable has one right way

The USB cable's square end fits into the Arduino only one way. If it does not go in, do not push harder — you will break the connector. Flip the plug over and try again.

If something stops working

Stop pressing buttons. Look at your wiring. If you cannot see what is wrong, call the teacher — this is what the stuck protocol (R2) is for. You will not be blamed for wiring something wrong. Wiring things wrong is part of learning Arduino.
