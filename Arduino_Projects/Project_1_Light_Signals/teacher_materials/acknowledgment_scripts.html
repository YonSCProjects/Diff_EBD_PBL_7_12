Acknowledgment Scripts

Robotics Workshop · Agourim School · prepared sentences + dosing guidance
Structured acknowledgment is not spontaneous praise. It is a short prepared sentence, delivered at a decided moment, by a decided person, at a decided frequency, with no lingering and no follow-up inspection. Use this card with the student's Anchor Log (Section 5 — Structured Acknowledgment Scripts). The sentences below are starting points. Adapt wording per student; record the final sentence in the log.
The four dosing rules
  • Consistent — every time the anchor moment happens, not just when we feel like it.
  • Coordinated — one designated person delivers it per student per day. Not every adult who walks past.
  • Brief — one sentence, then walk on. Not a conversation. Not a question. Not an inspection of the work.
  • Without piling on — no "and also…", no "next time you could…", no extra tasks appended. The sentence stands alone.

Language to avoid

Don't pair acknowledgment with correction. A sentence that starts with "great, but…" is not an acknowledgment — it is a correction dressed up as praise, and students hear the correction. Save corrections for a separate moment.
AvoidUse instead
"Finally!""Good to see you."
"Why can't you do this every time?"(nothing — the acknowledgment stands alone)
"Great, now imagine if you'd done this last week.""That took focus. I noticed."
"See? You can do it when you want to.""I appreciate that you did this on your own."
"Great job — now let's do the next one.""That took focus." (walk on; the next task lives on the card)
