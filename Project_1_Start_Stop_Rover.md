# Robotics Project 1: The "Start-Stop" Rover
*A 100% Verified Differentiated PBL Module for EBD/ADHD/ODD*

**Objective:** Build a simple two-wheel rover that moves forward for 3 seconds and stops. 
*Why this project?* It focuses on a highly achievable, tangible outcome to build immediate trust and provide a quick dopamine hit, preventing the frustration of complex open-ended builds.

## The Setup & "Wait Protocol" Readiness
*Prep before the students enter the room.*
1. **The Traffic Light Cups:** Each student's station has a Red, Yellow, and Green cup. 
2. **The Default Backup Task (Red Cup):** On each desk is a small bin of mixed LEGOs or bolts. *Instruction:* "If your cup is Red and I am busy, your job is to sort these pieces by color or size until I get there." 
   * *[Verification: Minahan & Rappaport (2012) - Prevents panic/paralysis from unstructured wait time].*
3. **Visual Milestone Cards:** Do not give verbal multi-step instructions. Give each student a physical card with just the current milestone. 
   * *[Verification: Berrezueta-Guzmán & Pau (2021) - The "Non-Human Mediator" removes oppositional triggers].*

---

## 15-Minute Micro-Milestones (Hyper-Chunking)
*These strictly adhere to the 15-minute cycle to combat executive dysfunction [Barkley, 2015].*

### Milestone 1: The Core Build (Collaborative Autonomy)
**The Choice:** "We need a chassis (base) for the motors. You can either use this ready-made template plate, OR you can build your own custom shape out of LEGO/parts, as long as it holds two motors."
* *[Verification: Greene (2014) - Bypassing ODD via choice-based autonomy].*
* **Check-out:** You spend 2 minutes checking their choice, then leave them to work for 10-15 minutes.

### Milestone 2: Plugging it In (The Checklist is Boss)
**The Checklist:** Provide a step-by-step visual diagram (or 30-second looping video on a tablet) showing the left motor plugging into Port A, and right motor into Port B.
* **Teacher Interaction:** "What does the picture show for the left wheel?" 
* *[Verification: Papakostas & Sidiropoulos (2021) - Deflecting authority to the diagram reduces interpersonal anxiety].*

### Milestone 3: Tangible Coding (Physical-to-Digital Interleaving)
**The Code Snippet:** Do not make them code from scratch. Provide a 3-line block of code on their screen:
1. `Start Motors A and B at 50% speed`
2. `Wait 3 Seconds`
3. `Stop Motors`
**The Task:** They plug in the robot and hit "Run". 
* *[Verification: Israel et al. (2015) - Explicit instruction prevents inquiry-based paralysis].*
* *[Verification: Cibrian & Lakes (2021) - Seeing the physical wheel spin provides immediate tangible feedback for self-regulation and ADHD focus].*

### Milestone 4: Differentiated Output & Tweaking
**The Task:** Ask them to change *one number* in the code (e.g., speed to 100%, or time to 5 seconds) and predict what happens.
- **For OCD/Perfectionism:** Emphasize that making the robot spin in a circle by accident is a "cool discovery," not a failure.
- **For Depression/Low Energy:** If they only finished Milestone 3 the entire session, celebrate heavily. "You got the wheels moving! Milestone 3 is a huge win today."
* *[Verification: Tomlinson (2014) - Process over product; adjusting expectations to readiness].*
