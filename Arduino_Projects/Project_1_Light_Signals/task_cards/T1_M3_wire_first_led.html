Wire your first external LED

Project 1 • Light Signals
You are going to wire an LED on the breadboard for the first time. This is hardware only — no sketch upload in this milestone. The sketch comes next, at Milestone 4.

What to do

  • Pick one LED from your parts tray. Any colour is fine.
  • Look at the LED's two legs. The long leg is positive (+) and the short leg is negative (−). Remember which is which.
  • Pick a 220 Ω resistor from the parts tray. The colour-code bands are red · red · brown.
  • Plant the LED across two rows of the breadboard so the legs land in different rows (not the same row — that would short them together).
  • Connect the LED's long leg (+) to the Arduino's digital pin 9, going through the 220 Ω resistor. The resistor sits between the LED's long leg and pin 9.
  • Connect the LED's short leg (−) to the Arduino's GND pin using a jumper wire.

Wiring diagram

What you should see The LED is in your breadboard with the long leg going through the resistor to pin 9, and the short leg going to GND. The LED is not lit yet — you have not uploaded a sketch that turns it on. That is Milestone 4. Right now you are just checking the wiring is correct.
Done when
  • The LED is planted in the breadboard with its two legs in different rows
  • The long leg connects to pin 9 through a 220 Ω resistor
  • The short leg connects to GND
  • The teacher (or you, checking against reference card R1) has confirmed the wiring is correct
Stuck? Check reference card R1 (Wiring Reference) first — it shows exactly how the LED should look in the breadboard. If you are still unsure which leg is long or which hole connects to pin 9, call the teacher. This is the first wiring milestone and the teacher will help you.
