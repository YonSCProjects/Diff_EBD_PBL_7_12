R1 — Wiring Reference

Project 1 • Light Signals • Student workstation card

This card shows you how to wire the LEDs, the resistors, and the push-button for Project 1. Keep it next to your breadboard. If you get confused about wiring, look at this card first before you call the teacher.

The most important rules

LED long leg = positive (+). It goes to the resistor, then to the Arduino pin.
LED short leg = negative (−). It goes to the Arduino GND.
Every LED needs its own 220 Ω resistor (colour bands: red · red · brown). No resistor = burnt LED.
The button needs a 10 kΩ pull-down resistor (colour bands: brown · black · orange). Without it the button reads random values.

Wiring 3 — Adding the button (Milestone 7)

The push-button straddles the centre gap of the breadboard so its four pins land in four different rows.

Arduino 5 V ──────────────┬─── Button pin A │ ▼ Button pin B ────┬─── Arduino pin 2 │ └─[10 kΩ]── Arduino GND

Important: the 10 kΩ pull-down resistor goes between pin 2 and GND, not between 5 V and GND. If you wire it between 5 V and GND, pressing the button creates a short circuit and the Arduino will reset.

Image slot — Wiring 3 (two LEDs + button with 10 kΩ pull-down on pin 2)
What the image should show:
  • Arduino Uno + breadboard with two LEDs already wired (from Wiring 2)
  • Tactile push-button straddling the centre gap of the breadboard
  • Button pin A → Arduino 5 V
  • Button pin B → Arduino digital pin 2 AND via 10 kΩ resistor → Arduino GND
  • The 10 kΩ pull-down resistor clearly positioned between pin 2 and GND, NOT between 5 V and GND
  • This is a pull-DOWN configuration, NOT pull-up
