This is a together-milestone. The teacher is next to you for this whole milestone. You do not work on it alone. If the teacher is with another student right now, wait at your workstation and work on sorting the resistor pack on your desk. The teacher will come to you.
This is your first milestone of the whole Arduino program. In this milestone you will pick a nickname for your folder (if you don't already have one), create your Project 1 folder together with the teacher, open Claude Code, plug in the Arduino, and see the little green light on the Arduino blink for the first time. It takes about 5 to 10 minutes.

What to do (with the teacher beside you)

  • First time in the workshop? The teacher will ask what nickname you want to use for your folder. You can pick anything you like — a character, an animal, your real first name, a favourite word. It will be your folder's name for the whole year, so pick something you like seeing.
  • Open File Explorer on the PC (press Windows key + E, or click the folder icon on the taskbar).
  • Go to this folder: G:\My Drive\Arduino_Projects\
  • Find your nickname folder, or create it together with the teacher if it does not exist yet.
  • Inside your nickname folder, create a new folder called Project_1_Light_Signals.
  • Open Claude Code with your new Project_1_Light_Signals folder as its working directory. The teacher will help you.
  • Plug the Arduino Uno into the PC using the USB cable (square end into the Arduino, flat end into the PC).
  • Look at the Arduino board. Find the small green "L" LED near pin 13. It should be blinking on and off about once per second.
  • Open the Arduino IDE (the shortcut is on the desktop). Check that the Board menu shows "Arduino Uno" and the Port menu shows a COM port.

This is your folder, this is your project, Project 1 starts now.

What you should see The little green "L" LED near pin 13 on your Arduino is blinking on and off. This is the factory sketch that comes on every new Arduino — you have not uploaded anything yet. The blinking light proves the Arduino is working and the USB connection is good.
Done when
  • You have your own nickname folder at G:\My Drive\Arduino_Projects\<your_nickname>\
  • You have a new Project_1_Light_Signals folder inside it
  • Claude Code is open and pointed at your Project 1 folder
  • The Arduino is plugged in with the USB cable
  • The green "L" LED on the Arduino is blinking
Stuck? This is a together-milestone — the teacher will walk you through it. If the teacher is with another student, wait and work on the resistor-sorting backup task on your desk. The teacher will come to you.
