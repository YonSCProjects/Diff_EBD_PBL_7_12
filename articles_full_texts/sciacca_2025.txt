NOTES ON CONTENT (based on title and contextual sources):
The dissertation investigates the experiences of teachers who use project-based learning (PBL) as a pedagogical approach with students who have emotional and behavioral disorders (EBD). The title suggests a qualitative or mixed-methods approach examining teacher perspectives on implementing PBL in the context of EBD populations.

RELATED LITERATURE CONTEXT:
A related dissertation by O'Quinn (2025) investigated teachers' behavioral intervention experiences with EBD students in inclusive classrooms using hermeneutic phenomenology, revealing themes of "finding what works," "collaboration is key," and "self-efficacy shaped through experiences." The Sciacca dissertation likely addresses similar themes through the specific lens of PBL as a pedagogical strategy.

Key context from related research:
- Students with EBD have the worst academic outcomes compared to other disability categories (Lipscomb et al., 2017; Sutherland & Wehby, 2001; Trout et al., 2003)
- Many teachers feel overwhelmed and under-supported in implementing effective interventions for EBD students
- Project-based learning has shown potential for improving engagement, behavior, and outcomes for students with EBD (see related research by Taylor & Allen, Semantic Scholar)
