Project 1 • Light Signals
In this milestone you combine all your choices into a "signature pattern" — your own version of Project 1. At the end you show it to the teacher.

Step 1 — Decide your signature pattern

Write down, in your own words, what you want your final pattern to do:

My signature pattern is going to: _________________________________________________ _________________________________________________ _________________________________________________ _________________________________________________

Step 2 — Build it

  • Make sure your LEDs and button are all still wired correctly from Milestones 3 and 4.
  • Use Claude Code to modify your sketch so the final pattern matches your description.
  • Upload, watch, iterate. If it is not quite right, go back to Claude Code with a new (a)(b)(c) and try again.
  • Keep going until your pattern matches what you wanted — or until you decide the way it actually came out is better than what you planned.

Step 3 — Show it to the teacher

  • Call the teacher when you are happy with your signature pattern.
  • Show them the pattern running. Say in one or two sentences what you built and what the button does.
  • If you want, ask the teacher to take a photo of your finished Project 1 build. You can use the photo later to show family or friends.
What you should see Your own version of Project 1 running on your breadboard — LEDs blinking in the pattern you designed, button doing what you chose, colours you picked. The teacher has seen it and you have a photo (if you wanted one).
🎉 You just completed Project 1! You picked a pattern, modified code with Claude Code, wired a button, chose a behaviour, and built something that is your own design.
Done when
  • Your signature pattern runs on your breadboard
  • The button does what you chose
  • You showed the teacher and (optionally) got a photo
Stuck? If you cannot decide what your signature pattern should be, try combining two of the patterns you liked from Milestones 2 and 3 — for example, "alternating, but faster than the default, and the button toggles it on and off." That counts as a signature pattern. The point of this milestone is ownership, not novelty.
