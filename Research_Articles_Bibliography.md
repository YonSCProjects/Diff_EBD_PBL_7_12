# Research Articles and Professional Resources Bibliography
*Verified foundations for Differentiated Project-Based Learning (PBL) for Students with Emotional and Behavioral Disorders (EBD) in STEM/Robotics*

## Foundational & Verified Resources

1. **Belland, B. R., et al. (2017).** "Synthesizing Results from Empirical Research on Computer-Based Scaffolding in STEM Education: A Meta-Analysis." *Review of Educational Research*.
   - *Relevance:* Provides empirical evidence on how scaffolding reduces cognitive load and helps students with executive functioning challenges succeed in STEM.

2. **Basham, J. D., & Marino, M. T. (2013).** "Understanding STEM Education and Supporting Students through Universal Design for Learning." *Teaching Exceptional Children*.
   - *Relevance:* A cornerstone article on applying UDL guidelines specifically to STEM environments to support cognitive and neurodivergent diversity.

3. **Israel, M., et al. (2015).** "Supporting all learners in school-wide computational thinking: A cross-case qualitative agency teaching study." *Computers & Education*.
   - *Relevance:* Investigates practical ways to integrate students with disabilities into computer science and robotics, highlighting the need for explicit instruction within open-ended tasks.

4. **CAST (2018).** *Universal Design for Learning Guidelines version 2.2*.
   - *Relevance:* The definitive, research-based framework for designing learning environments that are accessible to all, focusing on multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression.

5. **Tomlinson, C. A. (2014).** *The Differentiated Classroom: Responding to the Needs of All Learners (2nd Ed.).* ASCD.
   - *Relevance:* The leading text on Differentiated Instruction (DI), providing strategies for differentiating content, process, and product based on student readiness and interest.

6. **Minahan, J., & Rappaport, N. (2012).** *The Behavior Code: A Practical Guide to Understanding and Teaching the Most Challenging Students.* Harvard Education Press.
   - *Relevance:* Breaks down internalizing (anxiety, depression) and externalizing (ODD, ADHD) behaviors and provides step-by-step intervention plans, such as predictable routines and break-cards.

7. **Greene, R. W. (2014).** *Lost at School: Why Our Kids with Behavioral Challenges are Falling Through the Cracks and How We Can Help Them.* Scribner.
   - *Relevance:* Outlines the Collaborative & Proactive Solutions (CPS) model, demonstrating why rigid power dynamics trigger ODD and how mutual problem-solving fosters independence.

8. **Barkley, R. A. (2015).** *Attention-Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder: A Handbook for Diagnosis and Treatment (4th Ed.).* Guilford Press.
   - *Relevance:* The definitive resource on ADHD as a disorder of executive functioning and self-regulation, emphasizing the need for externalized scaffolding (timers, physical models).

9. **Condliffe, B., et al. (2017).** *Project-Based Learning: A Literature Review.* MDRC.
   - *Relevance:* A comprehensive review of PBL implementation, noting the structural challenges it presents and the necessary supports required for diverse learners.

10. **Hwang, J., & Taylor, J. C. (2016).** "Stemming on STEM: A STEM Education Framework for Students with Disabilities." *Journal of Science Education for Students with Disabilities*.
    - *Relevance:* Discusses specific pedagogical adjustments necessary to make hands-on STEM education accessible and emotionally manageable for special education populations.
