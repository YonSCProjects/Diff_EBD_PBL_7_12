Abstract (from Sage Journals):
[Full abstract not available without subscription. Based on DOI page and thesis, article describes:]
This study evaluated the effect of technology-based self-monitoring using the I-Connect application to increase on-task behavior for students with or at risk for emotional and behavioral disorders (EBD). A non-concurrent multiple baseline design across participants was used. Participants were upper-level elementary school students in a self-contained classroom. Results demonstrated significant increases in on-task behavior as a result of the I-Connect plus reinforcement intervention.

Based on precursor USF thesis (2022, same study):
The purpose of this study was to extend the findings from Clemons et al. (2016) and examine student and teacher use of a web-based self-monitoring application, I-Connect, and reinforcement to increase on-task behavior for upper-level elementary school students with or at risk for an emotional behavioral disorder (EBD) using extended self-monitoring intervals. Significant increases in on-task behavior as a result of the I-Connect plus reinforcement intervention in the classroom were demonstrated for all three participants.

Participants:
- Three upper-level elementary school students (Cayson, Juan, Michael) in a self-contained ESE classroom
- Cayson: language and speech impairment, specific learning disability, ADHD, disruptive mood dysregulation disorder, at risk for EBD
- Juan: 10-year-old diagnosed with EBD and ADHD
- Michael: disruptive off-task behavior

Design: Non-concurrent multiple baseline across participants
Setting: Public elementary school in Florida, self-contained 4th-5th grade classroom

Key findings (from thesis precursor):
- Significant increases in on-task behavior for all three participants with I-Connect + reinforcement
- Treatment integrity was 100% across all participants
- Strong social validity for students and teachers

Keywords: self-monitoring, I-Connect, EBD, on-task behavior, technology, single-case design, positive behavior interventions
