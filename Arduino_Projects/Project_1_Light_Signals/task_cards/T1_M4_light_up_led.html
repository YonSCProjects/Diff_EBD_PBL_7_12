Project #1 — Light Signals: Milestone 4 of 8

🏁Upload the sketch and light up the LED

Project 1 • Light Signals
In this milestone you upload a sketch that blinks the LED you wired — the moment "something I wired" responds to "code I uploaded."

📋What to do

  • Open the file 02_blink_external.ino in the Arduino IDE.It is in your Project 1 folder under ino_files/02_blink_external/.
  • Click the Upload button (the right-arrow in the toolbar).
  • Wait for "Done uploading" to appear at the bottom of the IDE in green.
  • Look at the LED on your breadboard. It should be blinking on and off about once per second.
  • If it is not blinking, run the stuck protocol at the bottom of this card before calling the teacher.

💻The sketch you are uploading

02_blink_external.ino// Blinks the external LED wired to digital pin 9.

const int LED_PIN = 9;

void setup() {
  pinMode(LED_PIN, OUTPUT);
}

void loop() {
  digitalWrite(LED_PIN, HIGH);
  delay(500);
  digitalWrite(LED_PIN, LOW);
  delay(500);
}
👀What you should see Your external LED on the breadboard is blinking on and off about once per second. This is the moment code you uploaded makes something physical happen on your own wiring. Take a second to notice that the "L" LED on the Arduino board has stopped blinking fast — because you replaced that sketch with this one.
✅Done when
  • The external LED on your breadboard is blinking once per second
  • The Arduino IDE shows "Done uploading" in green
🪄Stuck? LED not blinking?
  • Check the LED orientation. The long leg (+) goes to the resistor and then to pin 9. The short leg (−) goes to GND. If they are backwards, flip the LED.
  • Check the resistor. The resistor must be in series — between pin 9 and the LED's long leg — not just stuck somewhere in the breadboard.
  • Check that Upload worked. Look for the green "Done uploading" message at the bottom of the IDE. Red error text means the upload did not happen.
  • If still stuck, call the teacher.
