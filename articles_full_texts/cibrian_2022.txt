ARTICLE: The Potential for Emerging Technologies to Support Self-Regulation in Children with ADHD: A Literature Review

AUTHORS: Franceli Linney Cibrian; Kimberley D. Lakes; Sabrina E. B. Schuck; Gillian R. Hayes

JOURNAL: International Journal of Child-Computer Interaction (IJCCI)
VOLUME: 31
ARTICLE NUMBER: 100421
YEAR: 2022 (published online November 2021)
DOI: 10.1016/j.ijcci.2021.100421
Semantic Scholar Corpus ID: 243462404

ABSTRACT/SUMMARY:
This review aims to provide a resource to summarize the current evidence for technological interventions that assist and assess the self-regulation of behaviors in children with ADHD. Thirty-six articles were reviewed using various technologies, such as computers, mobile devices, wearables and sensors, virtual and augmented reality, and robotics.

KEY FINDINGS (from CHADD Research Update summary and secondary sources):
- Mobile health devices have shown promising results in terms of assessing emotions, helping with organization, and enhancing self-monitoring.
- Research on the use of sensors and wearables is more limited but suggests that these devices may be able to detect behaviors and give feedback to children to help them with behavior and self-regulation.
- Studies on games that have the purpose of improving self-regulation appear promising in training self-regulation and collaborative skills. These serious games have been delivered by personal computers, mobile technology, sensors, and robots.
- Little research exists on using augmented or virtual reality to increase self-regulation and decrease ADHD symptoms; however, existing preliminary studies suggest its possibility and potential.
- Although limited studies exist on computer software and web-based applications for children with ADHD, usage suggests potential applications.
- More studies exist on neurofeedback and cognitive training, with less research on emerging technologies for interventions for ADHD.
- Given the increasing use of technology in daily life, better understanding how such technology can be used to help individuals with ADHD has widespread potential.
