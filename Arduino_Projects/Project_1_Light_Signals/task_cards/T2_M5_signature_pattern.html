This is the final milestone of Tier 2 Project 1. You have picked a pattern (Milestone 2), modified it with Claude Code (Milestone 3), added a button with a specific behaviour (Milestone 4). Now you combine all of that into a "signature pattern" — your own version of Project 1, with your colour choices, your timing, your button behaviour. At the end you show it to the teacher and they take a photo for your portfolio (if you want one).

Step 2 — Build it

  • Make sure your LEDs and button are all still wired correctly from Milestones 3 and 4.
  • Use Claude Code Level 2 (or Level 3 if you feel confident) to modify your sketch so the final pattern matches your description.
  • Upload, watch, iterate. If it is not quite right, go back to Claude Code with a new (a)(b)(c) and try again.
  • Keep going until your pattern matches what you wanted — or until you decide the way it actually came out is better than what you planned.

Step 3 — Show it to the teacher

  • Call the teacher when you are happy with your signature pattern.
  • Show them the pattern running. Say in one or two sentences what you built and what the button does.
  • If you want, ask the teacher to take a photo of your finished Project 1 build. You can use the photo later to show family or friends.

Stuck? If you cannot decide what your signature pattern should be, try combining two of the patterns you liked from Milestones 2 and 3 — for example, "alternating, but faster than the default, and the button toggles it on and off." That counts as a signature pattern. The point of this milestone is ownership, not novelty.
