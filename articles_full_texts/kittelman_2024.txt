CICO-Secondary: Enhancing High School Behavior Support

This study evaluates the implementation of an adapted Check-in/Check-out (CICO-Secondary) intervention for high school students, focusing on its procedural fidelity and impact on academic engagement. Conducted with three ninth-grade students, the results indicate that CICO-Secondary can be effectively implemented and is associated with improved student engagement and perceived social validity. The findings suggest the need for further research to validate the intervention's effectiveness in typical high school settings.

 Abstract
Check-in/check-out (CICO) is an evidence-based multicomponent intervention widely implemented in elementary
and middle schools. The purpose of this study was to evaluate whether an adapted CICO intervention, with CICO
components designed to be more developmentally appropriate for adolescents and contextually relevant for high schools,
could be implemented with procedural fidelity and improve outcomes for students with and at risk for disabilities. The
study included three ninth-grade students (two with Individualized Education Programs) in one U.S. public high school.
We used a non-concurrent multiple baseline design conducted over two academic years during the COVID-19 pandemic
to experimentally evaluate the effects of CICO-Secondary on student academic engagement. Results demonstrated that
students participated with adequate procedural fidelity and that there was a functional relation between implementation
of CICO-Secondary and improved student academic engagement. Students and school personnel also perceived CICO-
Secondary to be socially valid. Implications for both future research and practice are discussed.

### How was the CICO-Secondary intervention adapted to meet the developmental needs of high school students, and what were the outcomes of this adaptation?

The CICO-Secondary intervention was adapted to be more developmentally appropriate for high school students by aligning it with the increased academic and social demands typical of this educational level. The outcomes showed that the intervention was implemented with procedural fidelity and led to improved student academic engagement, with a functional relation evident between the implementation of CICO-Secondary and increased engagement. To ensure procedural fidelity during the implementation of CICO-Secondary, the study used several strategies. Continuous observations were also conducted during the intervention phase to monitor classroom engagement and disruptions .

The high social validity ratings of the CICO-Secondary intervention can be attributed to several factors: the developmental appropriateness of the adapted components for high school students, the perceived usability and understandability of the electronic Daily Progress Report (eDPR), and the consistent support offered to students through feedback and self-monitoring practices. The adapted CICO-Secondary intervention addresses exclusionary discipline practices by increasing student engagement and reducing the likelihood of escalating challenging behaviors that often lead to disciplinary actions. By focusing on teaching self-regulation skills and fostering positive teacher-student interactions through structured check-ins and feedback, the intervention helps create a more supportive and inclusive school environment, reducing the reliance on exclusionary measures such as suspensions and disciplinary referrals .
