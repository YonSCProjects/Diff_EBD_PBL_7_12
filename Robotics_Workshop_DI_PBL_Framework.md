# Differentiated PBL Framework for EBD Robotics Workshop (4-8 Students)
*Grounded in Verified Special Education and Behavioral Psychology Research*

Objective: Design projects so students with severe ADHD, ODD, anxiety, depression, OCD, and PTSD can work independently at least some of the time, allowing you to cycle through the group.

## 1. The "Station & Milestone" Model (Based on Barkley, 2015 & CAST, 2018)
Since you are managing 4-8 different projects, you cannot be a verbal bottleneck for instructions. Students with ADHD/trauma have compromised working memory and need external scaffolding.
- **Visual Milestones:** Break every project into 5-10 micro-milestones. Provide these visually (a printed card or digital board). 
- **Universal Design (UDL):** Provide 1) a written checklist, 2) a visual schematic, and 3) a physical prototype of the current milestone so students can self-serve instructions in the format that best suits their neurotype.

## 2. Managing ODD & ADHD via Collaborative Autonomy (Based on Greene, 2014 & Tomlinson, 2014)
Students with ODD resist direct, rigid commands. Motivation must be choice-based to bypass oppositional power struggles.
- **If-Then Choices:** Frame tasks as choices over the *process*. "For this milestone, you can tackle coding the distance sensor, OR you can work on the motor alignment. Which do you want to tackle for the next 15 minutes?"
- **Leverage Hyperfocus (ADHD):** Find their specific interest within the project (coding vs. building vs. decorating) and let them hyperfocus on that while you help others.

## 3. The "Wait Protocol" for Anxiety and OCD (Based on Minahan & Rappaport, 2012)
Unstructured time or getting "stuck" when the teacher is busy is a primary trigger for panic, anxiety, and behavioral escalation.
- **Traffic Light / Output System:** Give students a clear way to signal they need help without verbalizing it (e.g., placing a red cup on their desk).
- **Default Backup Tasks:** Every student must have a continuous "low stress/high success" backup task available. If they get stuck and you are helping someone else, the rule is they immediately switch to the backup task (sorting parts, testing LEDs, drawing their design) until you arrive. This prevents paralysis or meltdown.

## 4. Differentiated Output and Explicit Instruction (Based on Israel et al., 2015 & Hwang & Taylor, 2016)
Inquiry-based learning requires too much cognitive leap for some neurodivergent learners without explicit instruction embedded.
- **Provide Code Snippets:** Instead of asking students with severe executive dysfunction or depression to code from scratch, provide functioning code snippets that they can tweak and modify. 
- **Process Over Product:** For OCD (perfectionism) or Depression (low energy), reduce the scope of the project. Celebrate a working prototype (even an ugly or highly simplified one) heavily. A robot that moves forward 3 inches is a massive success.

## 5. The 15-Minute Cycle 
Design tasks that require short, manageable bursts of independent work suited for compromised executive function.
- Spend 2-3 minutes with Student A to get them started on a micro-milestone.
- Move to Student B, C, and D.
- By the time you rotate back to Student A 10-15 minutes later, they have just finished their milestone and are ready for the next check-in.
