R1 — Wiring Reference

Project 1 • Light Signals • Student workstation card

This card shows you how to wire the LEDs, the resistors, and the push-button for Project 1. Keep it next to your breadboard. If you get confused about wiring, look at this card first before you call the teacher.

The most important rules

LED long leg = positive (+). It goes to the resistor, then to the Arduino pin.
LED short leg = negative (−). It goes to the Arduino GND.
Every LED needs its own 220 Ω resistor (colour bands: red · red · brown). No resistor = burnt LED.
The button needs a 10 kΩ pull-down resistor (colour bands: brown · black · orange). Without it the button reads random values.

Wiring 1 — One LED on pin 9 (Milestone 3)

Arduino pin 9 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED long leg │ ▼ LED short leg ─── Arduino GND

Wiring 2 — Two LEDs on pins 9 and 10 (Milestone 5)

Same as Wiring 1, but you add a second LED of a different colour one row over on the breadboard, with its own 220 Ω resistor going to pin 10.

Arduino pin 9 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED 1 long leg ▼ LED 1 short leg ─── Arduino GND Arduino pin 10 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED 2 long leg ▼ LED 2 short leg ─── Arduino GND

Wiring 3 — Adding the button (Milestone 7)

The push-button straddles the centre gap of the breadboard so its four pins land in four different rows.

Arduino 5 V ──────────────┬─── Button pin A │ ▼ Button pin B ────┬─── Arduino pin 2 │ └─[10 kΩ]── Arduino GND

Important: the 10 kΩ pull-down resistor goes between pin 2 and GND, not between 5 V and GND. If you wire it between 5 V and GND, pressing the button creates a short circuit and the Arduino will reset.

Wiring 4 — Three LEDs for the chasing pattern (Tier 2 only)

Only needed if you chose the chasing pattern in Tier 2. Add a third LED on pin 11 with its own 220 Ω resistor, wired the same way as LEDs 1 and 2.

Arduino pin 11 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED 3 long leg ▼ LED 3 short leg ─── Arduino GND

Wiring 5 — Three LEDs + button (Tier 2 Milestone 4 — Option B)

Only needed if you chose the chasing pattern in Tier 2 and are adding the button in Milestone 4. Combines Wiring 4 (three LEDs on pins 9, 10, 11) with Wiring 3 (button on pin 2 with 10 kΩ pull-down).

Arduino pin 9 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED 1 long leg ▼ LED 1 short leg ─── Arduino GND Arduino pin 10 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED 2 long leg ▼ LED 2 short leg ─── Arduino GND Arduino pin 11 ───[220 Ω]───┬─── LED 3 long leg ▼ LED 3 short leg ─── Arduino GND Arduino 5 V ──────────────┬─── Button pin A ▼ Button pin B ────┬─── Arduino pin 2 └─[10 kΩ]── Arduino GND

Same pull-down rule: the 10 kΩ resistor goes between pin 2 and GND, not between 5 V and GND.
