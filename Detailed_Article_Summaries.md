# Summary of Verified Resources for EBD Robotics Workshop

These summaries translate the verified, foundational research in Special Education, UDL, and behavioral psychology directly to your 4-8 student robotics workshop.

## 1. Executive Functioning and External Scaffolding (Barkley, 2015; Belland et al., 2017)
* **Research Concept:** Students with severe ADHD or trauma have impaired working memory and executive functioning. Open-ended PBL tasks easily overwhelm them.
* **Robotics Application:** Never give a multi-step instruction verbally. Externalize the working memory by using highly scaffolded visual checklists. Break the robot build into "micro-steps" (e.g., "Connect motor A to Port 1") so they can complete short autonomous bursts of work.

## 2. Universal Design for STEM (Basham & Marino, 2013; CAST, 2018)
* **Research Concept:** UDL requires presenting information in multiple formats to reduce barriers to entry naturally.
* **Robotics Application:** For each milestone in your workshop, provide 1) a physical, finished prototype of that specific part, 2) a short video loop showing the assembly, and 3) a written checklist. This caters to OCD (precision), ADHD (visual learning), and trauma (anxiety over doing it "wrong").

## 3. The Behavior Code for Anxiety and Depression (Minahan & Rappaport, 2012)
* **Research Concept:** Students with internalizing disorders (anxiety, PTSD) become paralyzed by unstructured time or when they make a mistake and the teacher is busy.
* **Robotics Application:** Establish a "Wait Protocol." When you are helping another student, an anxious student needs a predictable, low-stakes "backup task" they can default to automatically. Examples: sorting Lego pieces by color, untangling wires, or testing individual LEDs.

## 4. Bypassing ODD via Collaborative Autonomy (Greene, 2014; Tomlinson, 2014)
* **Research Concept:** Students with Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD) react to perceived control or demands with defiance. Motivation must be intrinsic and choice-based.
* **Robotics Application:** Differentiate the *process* and *product*. Frame tasks as choices over which the student has agency: "Today, we need to get the chassis built. You can either follow these instructions to build the standard one, or you can design your own custom shape for it as long as it fits the wheels."

## 5. Explicit Instruction in Computational Thinking (Israel et al., 2015; Hwang & Taylor, 2016)
* **Research Concept:** Inquiry-based learning often fails for students with disabilities without explicit, direct instruction embedded within the project.
* **Robotics Application:** While the overall project is "Build a robot," the coding aspect must be explicitly taught. Use pre-written "code snippets" that students can modify (e.g., changing the speed variable from 50 to 100) rather than asking them to write scripts entirely from scratch, ensuring high success rates and lowering frustration.
