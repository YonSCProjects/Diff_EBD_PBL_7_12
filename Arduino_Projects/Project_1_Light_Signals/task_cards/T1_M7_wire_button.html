🏁Wire the push-button

Project 1 • Light Signals
In this milestone you add a push-button and a pull-down resistor. The hardest wiring step of Project 1 — call the teacher if you are unsure.

📋What to do

  • Pick a tactile push-button from your parts tray. It has four pins.
  • Plant the button on the breadboard across the centre gap so its four pins land in four different rows (not all in the same row). If it is lying flat, the orientation needs to change — rotate it 90 degrees.
  • Pick a 10 kΩ resistor from the parts tray. The colour-code bands are brown · black · orange. (Different from the 220 Ω LED resistor!)
  • Connect one side of the button (pin A) to the Arduino's 5 V pin.
  • Connect the other side of the button (pin B) to the Arduino's digital pin 2.
  • From pin B, also connect through the 10 kΩ resistor to GND. This is the pull-down resistor — it holds pin 2 at 0 V when the button is not pressed.

🔌Wiring diagram

Arduino 5 V ──────────────┬─── Button pin A
                          │
                          ▼
                      Button pin B ────┬─── Arduino pin 2
                                       │
                                       └─[10 kΩ]── Arduino GND
⚠️Important — the most common mistake The 10 kΩ pull-down resistor must go between pin 2 and GND. Not between 5 V and GND. If you put it between 5 V and GND, pressing the button creates a short circuit and the Arduino will reset itself. This is the single most common wiring mistake in Project 1. Before you plug anything back in, trace the resistor with your finger: does it go from pin 2 to GND? If yes, you are safe. If it goes from 5 V to GND, that is the fix — move the resistor to pin 2 → GND. Call the teacher if you would like help with that.
👀What you should see The button is planted across the centre gap of the breadboard. One side of it goes to 5 V. The other side goes to pin 2, and also through the 10 kΩ resistor to GND. The two LEDs on pins 9 and 10 are still wired. Nothing new happens yet when you press the button — the current sketch does not know about the button. The next card changes that.
✅Done when
  • Button is on the breadboard, straddling the centre gap
  • Button pin A connects to 5 V
  • Button pin B connects to pin 2
  • 10 kΩ resistor sits between pin 2 and GND (the pull-down)
  • The two LEDs on pins 9 and 10 are still wired and still alternating
🪄Stuck?
  • This is the hardest wiring step of Project 1. The teacher expects to help at this milestone — call them.
  • If the Arduino keeps resetting or turning off, you almost certainly have the pull-down on the wrong side — unplug the USB, fix the resistor, plug back in.
